Balearic Islands


This archipielago in the western Mediterranean sea is one of the most popular holiday destination by Europeans.

From top summer club scene of Ibiza, to one of the best beaches in the world, Ses Illetes - Formentera, the Balearic Islands offer a wide range of atmospheres for all tastes. 

The Cyclades


Greece is famous all over the world for its more than 220 stunning islands full of beaches with pristine water.

The name "Cyclades" refers to the islands forming a circle around the sacred Delos. Their architecture is composed of sugar houses with flowered gardens, white walls, blue-domed churches, and paved paths.

Superb nightlife


Discover the many faces of the Mediterranean nightlife: from chill out at luxury hotels bars, to retro and endless festivities on wild beaches.

Driven by a cheerful atmosphere, it's full of parties and special events brightened by the moonlight, keeping you alive until the first sunlight.

Mediterranean cuisine 


Mediterranean cuisine has a culinary tradition of some 4000 years, founded on the "Mediterranean triad" (wheat, olive oil and wine) and influenced by the cuisine of Turkey, the Balkans, Italy and the Levant.

Each region has its own kitchen, although fresh and seasonal products are the common pillars everywhere.
